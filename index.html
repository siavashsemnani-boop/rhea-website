The story behind Rhea
Made by hand.
Driven by
obsession.

It began before she had words for it. At five years old, in Tehran, she was collecting scraps of fabric — whatever she could find — and turning them into something wearable. Not playing dress-up. Making clothes. There was no school for it yet, no Instagram, no name for what she was doing. Just a child and her instinct for cloth.

"I didn't learn to love fabric.
I was born already in love with it."

Ten years ago, in a small flat in Tehran, RHEA became real. No atelier, no team — just the same obsession from childhood, grown up and made precise. She opened an Instagram account and began building from nothing, piece by piece, stitch by stitch. Each garment shared was an act of courage. Each follower, a stranger who understood something.

A decade of work. A decade of learning what fabric can say when a person listens closely enough. The mirror-work, the brocade, the organza — these are not decorations. They are the language of a culture she carries with her everywhere she goes.

Now, from Tehran to Berlin, RHEA is ready for the world. Not because the work changed — but because she finally believes it always deserved to be seen.
